App by app

Facts only. No affiliate links. Last reviewed June 2026.

soulyap

One confession. One match. No feed.

Write one honest confession anonymously. It is matched to one past confession from someone who felt the same, plus a count of how many others shared that feeling. No public feed, no comments, no DMs, no way for anyone to reply to you. Safety review runs on every submission before it can be matched or read.

Best for: people who want to feel less alone without performing for an audience.
Not for: getting replies, building followers, or public conversation.

Whisper

Public anonymous feed with comments.

Confessions are posted as image cards to a public feed, discoverable by everyone. Other users can comment publicly or send private messages. Moderated but community-visible by design.

Best for: broadcasting to a large community, reading others' confessions, public conversation.
Not for: strictly private sharing — posts are public.

NGL

Anonymous questions sent to YOUR Instagram.

You share an NGL link on your Instagram Story. Followers (or anyone with the link) send you anonymous questions. You receive them; you decide what to post publicly. Requires an Instagram account. Monetises via hints about who sent the message.

Best for: creators/influencers wanting anonymous audience Q&A.
Not for: confessing something privately — messages go to a specific named person.

Sendit

Anonymous messages via your Snapchat.

Similar to NGL but Snapchat-native. Share a link in your Snap Story; friends send anonymous messages that appear in your Sendit inbox. The app monetises via hints. Requires Snapchat.

Best for: getting anonymous feedback/messages from Snapchat contacts.
Not for: sharing your own confession privately.

Yik Yak

Local anonymous feed with upvotes + comments.

Posts are shown to users within a few miles. Fully anonymous, with upvotes and comments. Popular on college campuses. Moderated for harassment; still a fully public local feed.

Best for: hyper-local anonymous conversation, campus culture.
Not for: private emotional release — everything is public to nearby users.

Common questions

Is there an anonymous app with no replies and no feed?

Yes — soulyap. There is no feed, no comments, and no reply channel. You write one confession; it is matched to one similar past confession. Nothing is posted publicly.

Which anonymous app is best for feeling less alone?

soulyap is built specifically for this use case. The match feature shows you a past confession from someone who felt the same thing, plus a count of how many others did — without broadcasting your words to a public audience.

Are NGL and Sendit the same as confession apps?

No — they are anonymous inbox tools. Someone else shares a link; you send them an anonymous message. They are not designed for confessing something privately; they deliver messages to a specific named person linked to their social account.

Which app has the strongest privacy design?

soulyap separates identity from confession at the source using a one-way cryptographic token — not even the operator can link a confession back to the account that wrote it. Whisper and Yik Yak store posts publicly. NGL/Sendit messages go to identifiable recipients.

Does soulyap have ads or sell data?

No ads. The only monetisation is an optional supporter subscription for unlimited reading. Writing, matching, and crisis resources are always free. No confession text is used for advertising or sold to third parties.
